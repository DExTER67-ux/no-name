Lunugamvehera National Park

About This Place

A buffer park connecting Yala and Udawalawe national parks, forming an important wildlife corridor for elephants moving between the two larger reserves. The park centres around the Lunugamvehera reservoir and offers good elephant and crocodile sightings in a much quieter setting than its more famous neighbours.

Best Time To Visit

February to September during the dry season when wildlife gathers around the reservoir and sightings are most reliable.

Quick Tips

  • Combine with Yala or Udawalawe for a full southern wildlife circuit
  • Much fewer visitors than neighbouring parks making for a peaceful experience
  • Book a jeep through your accommodation or at the park entrance
  • Morning and late afternoon are the best times for elephant sightings
  • Bring water and snacks as facilities inside the park are very limited
