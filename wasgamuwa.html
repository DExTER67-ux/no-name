Wasgamuwa National Park

About This Place

A quiet and less commercialised national park on the edge of the central highlands, home to large herds of elephants as well as leopards, sloth bears, and mugger crocodiles. The park's diverse habitats of riverine forest, grassland, and scrub give it excellent biodiversity while its relatively low visitor numbers mean a more peaceful and intimate safari experience.

Quick Tips

  • Far fewer visitors than Yala or Udawalawe making for a quieter experience
  • Book a jeep and guide through your accommodation or at the park entrance
  • Early morning and late afternoon safaris give the best wildlife sightings
  • The riverine areas are particularly good for crocodile and bird sightings
  • Bring enough water and snacks as facilities inside the park are minimal
