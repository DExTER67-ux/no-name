Embekke Devalaya

About This Place

A 14th century Hindu and Buddhist shrine famous for its extraordinarily detailed wooden carvings on the pillars of its drummers' hall. The intricate woodwork depicts mythical creatures, dancers, and wrestlers, representing some of the finest traditional Sri Lankan wood carving still surviving today.

Quick Tips

  • The wooden pillar carvings are the main highlight so take time to examine each one
  • Remove shoes before entering
  • The temple is smaller and quieter than major tourist sites
  • Combine with a visit to nearby Lankatilaka Temple
  • A local guide can help explain the symbolism in the carvings
