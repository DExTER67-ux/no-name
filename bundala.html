Bundala National Park

About This Place

A UNESCO designated Ramsar wetland site on the southern coast, internationally recognised for its importance as a wintering ground for migratory birds. The park's coastal lagoons, salt marshes, and scrub forest attract flamingos, painted storks, pelicans, and hundreds of other bird species, making it the top birdwatching destination in Sri Lanka.

Best Time To Visit

November to March when migratory birds arrive from as far as Siberia, significantly increasing the number and variety of species visible in the park.

Quick Tips

  • Bring binoculars as this is primarily a birdwatching destination
  • Early morning visits offer the most active birdlife
  • A guide with knowledge of bird species adds greatly to the experience
  • Combine with a day trip to nearby Yala National Park
  • The lagoon areas are best explored slowly by jeep to avoid disturbing the birds
