Maduru Oya National Park

About This Place

A remote and rarely visited national park in the dry zone, part of a network of protected areas that form one of the largest wildlife corridors in Sri Lanka. The park is home to elephants, leopards, and a range of dry zone wildlife, and its isolation from major tourist routes makes it one of the most genuinely wild places in the country.

Quick Tips

  • One of the least visited parks in Sri Lanka so expect a truly remote experience
  • Arrange a jeep and guide well in advance as facilities are minimal
  • Bring all supplies including water and food for the full safari day
  • The park is best combined with other east zone destinations on a longer trip
  • A four wheel drive vehicle is recommended for the access roads
