Kumana National Park

About This Place

An extension of the Yala ecosystem on the east coast, known for its spectacular bird colonies that nest around the Kumana Mangrove lagoon each year. The park is considered one of Asia's most important bird sanctuaries, attracting thousands of nesting waterbirds including painted storks, night herons, and spoonbills, alongside elephants and leopards.

Quick Tips

  • Visit between April and July to see the nesting bird colonies at their peak
  • Bring binoculars and a zoom camera lens for the best bird photography
  • Combine with a stay in nearby Arugam Bay for a full east coast experience
  • A licensed guide is essential for navigating the park and spotting wildlife
  • The park is less visited than Yala so expect a quieter and more remote atmosphere
